Parts of Force

Line of Action: An Imaginary line that continues through the path of the force infinitely behind and in front of the force.
Magnitude: The value of a force. Measured in (lbs or kips).
Direction: The relationship between the line of action and a reference line. Ex/ up or down, left or right.
Sense: Defines whether the force is POS(+) or NEG(-).
​
STRUCTURES CONVENTION: UP or RIGHT is POS(+) and DOWN or LEFT is NEG(-)

Moment

Moment:The effectiveness of a force to produce rotation about an axis.
STRUCTURES CONVENTION: CLOCKWISE is POSITVE (+) & COUNTER-CLOCKWISE is NEGATIVE(-)

Equilibrium

Equilibrium: When all forces acting upon a body produce no change in the motion of the body.
Static Equilibrium: When the body is not in motion and all of the forces acting on it cancel each other out.
Dynamic Equilibrium: When the body is stationary in the horizontal and vertical directions but is spinning it is in Dynamic Equilibrium
​

Stresses

Stress:The forces acting internally upon a body to counter balance the external forces.
Axial Stress:: Responses to external forces which act perpendicular to the cross-sectional plane of the beam.
Tensile Stress:Stress in response to a force pulling on a body.
Compresive Stress:Stress in response to a force pushing on a body.


Tangential Stress:The forces acting internally upon a body to counter balance the external forces.
Shear:The forces acting internally upon a body to counter balance the external forces.
Torsional Stress:The forces acting internally upon a body to counter balance the external forces.

Direct Stress Formula:The forces acting internally upon a body to counter balance the external forces.


Allowable Stress (F, PSI): The amount of stress a specific material can safely resist. It is a point below the yield strength on the stress strain diagram
​ Flexural Stress: The result of a horizontal beam supporting a downward load. When a beam bends downward, the top becomes shorter and the bottom becomes longer. Practically that means the top half is in compression and the bottom half in is tension. Thus, flexural stress is actually a combination of compression and tension.

Strain

Strain:External forces acting on a body cause internal stresses to develop which causes deformations called strain.
Hookes Law:The stress is directly proportional to the strain. Where Stress = k * Strain where k is a constant proportion
Modulus of Elasticity:The slope of the stress/strain curve in the elastic region and is expressed in psi.
Elongation:The tensile deformation within the elastic region
