Parts of Force

Line of Action: An Imaginary line that continues through the path of the force infinitely behind and in front of the force.
Magnitude: The value of a force. Measured in (lbs or kips).
Direction: The relationship between the line of action and a reference line. Ex/ up or down, left or right.
Sense: Defines whether the force is POS(+) or NEG(-).
​
STRUCTURES CONVENTION: UP or RIGHT is POS(+) and DOWN or LEFT is NEG(-)

Moment

Moment:The effectiveness of a force to produce rotation about an axis.
STRUCTURES CONVENTION: CLOCKWISE is POSITVE (+) & COUNTER-CLOCKWISE is NEGATIVE(-)

Equilibrium

Equilibrium: When all forces acting upon a body produce no change in the motion of the body.
Static Equilibrium: When the body is not in motion and all of the forces acting on it cancel each other out.
Dynamic Equilibrium: When the body is stationary in the horizontal and vertical directions but is spinning it is in Dynamic Equilibrium
