Types of Beams

Joists: Relatively small beams which are used in repetitive systems such as floors.
Rafters: Special type of joist used in sloping roofs.
Girders: Large beams which support smaller beams such as joists.

​

Types of Loads

Dead Loads: A load that is a permanent part of the beam.
Live Loads: A live load is a temporary load that will act on the beam.
Point Loads: When a load acts specifically at one spot on the beam.
Distributed Load: A load which acts uniformly across a beam.

Standard Live Loads
First Floor Residential Home 40 PSF
Temporary Platforms or Structures 50 PSF
Grandstands, Bleachers 100 PSF
Stage Floor 150 PSF

Tributary Area

Tributary Area: The area of a distributed load that is supported by a beam. For an “interior beam” this is the area defined by the distance from half way between the beam next to it on one side to half way between the next beam (s) by the length of the beam (l). For an “exterior beam” this area is defined by the distance from the beam to half way to the next beam by the length of the beam. This area multiplied can be multiplied by the Pounds/SQFT gives us the total load of the uniformly distributed load (W).

With the tributary area we can take a load given in PSF (pounds/square foot) and convert it into PLF (pounds/linear foot). We are able to represent the same amount of weight in an area in the top view as a line in the side view.

​

Reactions

Reaction: The external load on a beam that exerts an equal upward reaction to where beam is resting on.
  • A reaction is defined as the Sum of the Moments about a point. The point we will use is where the beam interacts with something holding it up. This will usually be a column, girder, or wire rope holding it up.
  • To check if the equation is correct take the sum of the vertical forces and it should equal zero. Because this will show that the problem is in equilibrium

Shear Diagram/h3> Shear Diagram: The Shear diagram shows where the stresses are in the beam. The Shear Diagram shows two important values.
Vmax: The maximum vertical shear is the greatest absolute value of shear in the diagram.
Vx = 0: x is the location where the maximum bending force will occur. (Mmax)
  • If Vx crosses zero multiple times Mmax will occur at one of those points
  • If Vx crosses zero at a point load x will equal the location where the point load acts.
  • If Vx crosses at a distributed load. You must solve for where Vx crosses zero.

Moment Diagram: Charts the bending moment (inlb or ftlb) versus the distance along the beam.

Parts of Force

Line of Action: An Imaginary line that continues through the path of the force infinitely behind and in front of the force.
Magnitude: The value of a force. Measured in (lbs or kips).
Direction: The relationship between the line of action and a reference line. Ex/ up or down, left or right.
Sense: Defines whether the force is POS(+) or NEG(-).
​
STRUCTURES CONVENTION: UP or RIGHT is POS(+) and DOWN or LEFT is NEG(-)
